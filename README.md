VITyarthi Project — Digital Literacy & Communication

This repository holds all the work I produced for the VITyarthi Digital Literacy & Communication program. The program ran across five tasks, each building on the last — starting from what digital literacy actually means and ending with a fairly eye-opening look at how cybercrime works in practice. I've tried to document everything clearly here so it's easy to navigate.

What This Project Covers
Task	Topic	What I Did
1	Introduction to Digital Literacy	Reflection on digital skills, tools, and responsible online behaviour
2	Evaluating Online Information	Source credibility, misinformation types, and the SIFT method
3	Online Privacy & Data Security	Digital footprint, password hygiene, and privacy settings audit
4	Email Etiquettes & Social Media Responsibility	Professional emails, Do's & Don'ts checklist, poor communication case study
5	Cybercrime Awareness	Phishing case study, prevention checklist, personal reflection
Module Summaries
Task 1 — Introduction to Digital Literacy
Explored what it actually means to be digitally literate — not just being able to use technology, but using it critically and responsibly. The key takeaway was that technical skill and digital literacy are not the same thing, and that distinction shaped everything that followed.

Task 2 — Evaluating Online Information
Learned how to assess whether a source is trustworthy using the SIFT method (Stop, Investigate the Source, Find Better Coverage, Trace Claims). Also covered the difference between misinformation, disinformation, and malinformation — surprisingly relevant for anyone working in AI, where data quality is everything.

Task 3 — Online Privacy & Data Security
Looked at how much data we generate passively without realising it. Covered practical password hygiene, two-factor authentication, app permission audits, and what a proper privacy settings review looks like. A lot of this was stuff I knew in theory but hadn't actually applied. Task 4 — Email Etiquettes & Social Media Responsibility
Part A — Wrote two professional emails: one requesting a deadline extension from a professor, another expressing interest in a summer internship at Reliance. Both follow correct email etiquette — formal tone, specific details, clear ask, polite close.

Part B — Built a social media Do's & Don'ts checklist aimed at college students. Covered things like not posting confidential information, not cyber-venting publicly, verifying before sharing, and understanding that deleted posts aren't actually gone.

Also wrote a critical reflection on a hypothetical scenario where a student's impulsive social media post cost them their entire internship season — and what they should have done instead.

Task 5 — Cybercrime Awareness
Part A — Deep-dived into phishing: how attacks are staged, who gets targeted, and the real consequences (financial loss, identity theft, academic harm). What genuinely surprised me was how little technical skill modern phishing actually requires — ready-made kits are available cheaply, which makes this a very accessible crime.

Part B — Created a 10-point "Stay Safe Online" checklist tailored for Indian college students, including two UPI-specific tips that I think most people don't know: QR codes can only send money, never receive it — "scan to receive" is always a scam.
